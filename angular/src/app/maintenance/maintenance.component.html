In Canada we get a 7-8 month window for our lawns on average. Below you will find a schedule outline you should follow during the warmer seasons in order to guarentee a healthy and happy lawn.

Lawn Prep (March - April)

Once the ground has dried out enough, you should prepare your lawn for seeding by doing the following:

  • Begin weeding your lawn and looking for insects on a semi-regular basis.
  • Dethatch your lawn to remove dead grass trapped within.
  • Aerate your lawn to loosen up the soil to promote better root growth for your grass.
  • Fertilize your lawn to stimulate more growth. (Please wait at least 2 weeks before seeding after fertilizing)

Spring Seeding (May - June)

Once the temperature is consistently between 15°C and 24°C you should overseed your lawn.

Mid-Summer Fertilization (July - August) *Optional

8 weeks after you seed, you may want to consider fertilizing your lawn again to ensure your grass has all the nutrients it needs.

Fall Fertilization (September - October)

To strengthen your lawn before the winter weather, fertilize your lawn to provide a surplus of nutrients.

Fall Seeding (September - October)

2 weeks after fall fertilization, seed your lawn once more to let new grassroots develop before winter sets in.

Lawn Mowing Tips

  • Never cut your grass shorter than 3 inches in height.
  • If your property gets a lot of sun, consider leaving your grass clippings on your lawn.
  • If your property has a lot of shade, consider bagging your grass clippings up.
